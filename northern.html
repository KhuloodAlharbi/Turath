Madain Saleh (AlUla)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient Nabatean tombs.

Madain Saleh, also known as Al-Hijr, is a historical archaeological site in AlUla. It contains well-preserved tombs carved into sandstone cliffs, similar to Petra in Jordan.

Tabuk Castle

An ancient fortress that has stood for centuries along the pilgrimage route.

Tabuk Castle is a historic site that dates back to the Ottoman era. It has played a key role in protecting travelers and pilgrims passing through the region.

Nafud Desert

A stunning desert landscape with golden sand dunes stretching for miles.

The Nafud Desert is one of the largest deserts in Saudi Arabia, famous for its towering dunes and unique rock formations. It is a popular destination for desert safaris and adventure seekers.

Dumat Al-Jandal Lake

A rare natural lake in the heart of the desert, offering scenic views and birdwatching opportunities.

Dumat Al-Jandal Lake is a beautiful freshwater lake in the northern region of Saudi Arabia. It attracts many migratory birds and is an ideal spot for nature lovers and photographers.
