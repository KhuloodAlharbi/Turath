Al-Masjid Al-Haram (Makkah)

The holiest site in Islam, home to the Kaaba, visited by millions annually for Hajj and Umrah.

Al-Masjid Al-Haram, also known as the Grand Mosque of Makkah, surrounds the Kaaba, the direction of prayer for all Muslims. It features stunning architecture, expansive prayer areas, and the iconic Black Stone.

Al-Masjid An-Nabawi (Madinah)

The Prophet Muhammad’s mosque, known for its green dome and spiritual significance.

Al-Masjid An-Nabawi is a major pilgrimage site built by Prophet Muhammad. It contains his tomb, beautiful courtyards, and an iconic retractable umbrella system in the courtyard area.

AlUla (Ancient City)

A historic city with breathtaking rock formations and UNESCO-listed heritage sites.

AlUla is home to Madain Saleh (Hegra), an ancient Nabatean city with rock-cut tombs and stunning desert landscapes, dating back thousands of years.

Red Sea Coast

A stunning coastal region with crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and pristine beaches.

The Red Sea Coast offers world-class diving, untouched coral reefs, and luxury resorts. It's a haven for marine life lovers and adventure seekers.
